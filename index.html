The National Library of Malaysia (NLM) is a library established under the National Library Act 1972 in Kuala Lumpur, Malaysia. The National Library is responsible for providing a collection of knowledge at national level for the present and future generations. In its effort to strengthen the library's collection, the National Library continues to play an active role in its acquisition of library materials through enforcement of the Deposit of Library Material Act 1986, acquisition, gift and exchange.

The pride of the National Library's collection is the Malaysian Collection. It comprises library materials published in Malaysia and overseas whose whole or larger part of the content is related to the publications date or the language used. Another national intellectual heritage is the Malay Manuscripts and one of her manuscripts, the Hikayat Hang Tuah has been acknowledged by UNESCO in the Memory of the World Register. It is a MS ISO 9001:2008 certified library.

Objectives
Objectives of National Library Malaysia

To ensure the availability of a national collection of library resources for the use of present and future generations

To facilitate nationwide access to library resources available within the country and abroad.

To provide leadership on matters pertaining to libraries.

To promote and provide access to the latest information and knowledge resources

Our Collections

Book Collection

Malaysian Monograph Collection is a collection of national heritage and Malaysian culture This collection includes materials published in Malaysia about Malaysia as well as materials published in other countries produced by Malaysian citizens, regardless of shape, language, script, or date generated.





Book Collection

General Monograph Collection is a large collection available at PNM's General Information Section. This collection includes foreign issues of reference monographs such as books, directories, dictionaries, encyclopedias, manuals, atlas, almanacs, thesaurus and Multimedia Reference Kit (RMK) from various subjects.





Serial Publication

Serial Publication General Collection includes printed and non-printed materials, as well as electronic and online materials. The Foreign serial publications are also available at the National Library of Malaysia. Which includes both past and current collections. Aside from that, bound volumes of newspaper articles are also available for reference.




Electronic Media Collections

It has been a pioneer in the development of the book industry in Malaysia by producing local content Audiobooks to meet the needs of the visually impaired. Authors and publishers have given National Library Malaysia copyright permission to convert the book from printed to audio format. In this published audio book, the reader's voice is provided by volunteers.

Private Publication

The National Library of Malaysia has acquired a private collection for conservation and reference purposes. The materials for field items are collected based on the collection owner's preferences, interests, or areas of interest. These private collections included contributions from A. Halim Nasir, Abdul Rahman Al-Ahmadi, Aziz Jahpin and A. Samad Said.





Rare Collection

includes material that was published before the 1950s and is difficult to find. It contains important information about religion, belief, and political background of Malay Land. PNM has developed rare collection in 1972. In these Rare Collections there are two types of collections, namely References and Research and Digital Copies.






Classified Collection

It consists of three materials namely Illegal Material, Restricted Material and Confidential Materials. Illegal material, the ministry of home affairs has published items that are prohibited from being distributed to the general public. For the restricted material, the publisher has limited distribution of previously published issues. For the Confidential Materials, Publications from government agencies, the commercial sector, and others that are not available to the general public.

Foreign Magazines or Journals Collection

The National Library of Malaysia has 486 magazines and foreign journals available as of October 2007. International Journal of Project Management, Child Abuse, AIDS Care, Fire Safety Journal, Learning and Instruction, The Journal of American Association, Time, Newsweek, The Economic, National Geographic, and Harvard Business Review are among the titles available for reference.
